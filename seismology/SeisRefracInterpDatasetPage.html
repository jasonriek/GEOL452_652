Seismology Refraction Interpretation Dataset

1) Interpretation of seismic refraction data will be the most difficult interpretation you have yet done.

2) Parameters, velocities and depths trade off, and you must develop systematic skills to be able to do this efficiently.

3) In this practice exercise, we will interpret data two ways. First, so that you get get a handle on how the data relate to the model you have generated we will import our travel time data into a spreadsheet and you will do the interpretation by hand, using the slope intercept method, as described in the notes.

4) After doing this, you will then reinterpret these data using an interpretation applet. In this exercise, you will see how absolute important it is to do your interpretation from the surface downward - not proceeding until you are absolutely certain about what you have done so far.
